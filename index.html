Literature Survey

Conducting a comprehensive literature survey is a fundamental step in any research project, and the "PlantSnap" initiative is no exception. The realm of plant disease and weed detection through machine learning has seen remarkable growth in recent years. Researchers such as Smith et al. (2019) demonstrated the potential of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) in identifying plant diseases with high accuracy, paving the way for more sophisticated models. Additionally, studies by Patel et al. (2020) explored the use of transfer learning techniques to improve the generalization of plant disease detection models, addressing a significant challenge in the field. However, the scarcity of comprehensive and diverse plant image datasets (Lin et al., 2019) remains a persistent concern, hindering the full potential of these models. The need for interpretable AI (Ribeiro et al., 2016) in this context is another crucial aspect highlighted in the literature, as it's essential for user trust and understanding. With the literature providing valuable insights and pointing out these gaps, the "PlantSnap" project aims to contribute by developing highly accurate ML models, addressing scalability, and improving real-time analysis while also collaborating to gather extensive datasets.

Research Overview and Research Gap

Our product centers around the analysis of factors influencing plant growth, Specifically, we have selected two factors, plant diseases and weeds, as focal points. Identifying diseases that can devastate entire crops and detecting weeds that compete for vital resources are pivotal concerns that can significantly impede agricultural productivity. Traditional solutions have proven insufficient in addressing these challenges. We introduce our innovative web application, a powerful tool designed to harness the capabilities of Weed and Plant disease identification. Our platform is tailored for large-scale agricultural operations, making it an ideal solution for commercial agriculture. Unlike standard mobile apps that handle individual images, our webApp enables bulk picture imports, providing extensive results that can be directly exported to an Excel sheet. This can be highlighted as a unique feature of our product. After the analysis is complete, the downloaded results can be stored.

This means that with access to past data, users can conveniently sort, filter, and visualize their data in ways that best suit their unique requirements. One of the significant benefits of our platform is its ability to identify weeds and diseases on a large scale. This not only aids in early pest and disease detection but also facilitates the precise calculation of pesticide and herbicide requirements, thereby optimizing their usage and saving costs. From an agricultural and financial perspective, this offers substantial benefits. Moreover, it enhances productivity, scalability, and environmental sustainability without consuming excessive time. Thus, this product doesn't just represent a step forward but rather a significant leap toward revolutionizing agriculture through technology.

Research Problem

The research problem at the heart of the "PlantSnap" project is the development of robust machine learning models for the identification of plant diseases and weeds, specifically tailored for large-scale agricultural and environmental applications. Existing studies have shown promise in leveraging techniques like CNNs and transfer learning, but they often fall short in terms of generalization, where the models struggle with real-world variations in plant conditions. Additionally, the scarcity of comprehensive and diverse plant image datasets is a persistent issue, limiting the breadth and accuracy of these models. Furthermore, the lack of interpretable AI hinders user trust and understanding of model decisions. These issues collectively form the core research problem we aim to address in "PlantSnap." We seek to create highly accurate ML models capable of robust identification while ensuring scalability and real-time analysis. We also aim to collaborate with experts and organizations to acquire diverse and extensive datasets. By tackling these challenges, we aim to make "PlantSnap" a valuable and accessible resource for effective plant health management.

Research Objectives

At PlantSnap, our research is driven by a clear set of objectives. We aim to develop highly accurate machine learning models that can effectively identify and differentiate various types of weeds and diseases in plants. These models are designed to deliver dependable and precise results, particularly when applied to large-scale agricultural and environmental settings. We are committed to creating a user-friendly web application, known as PlantSnap, which simplifies the process of capturing and uploading plant images for identification. This objective ensures that the identification process is accessible to a broad spectrum of users, including farmers, gardeners, and nature enthusiasts. We prioritize scalability, striving to make our ML models and the PlantSnap web app capable of efficiently handling a significant volume of plant images. Scalability is essential for meeting the ever-growing demands of the agricultural industry and environmental conservation efforts. In addition, we are dedicated to providing real-time analysis and diagnosis of plant health, thereby offering instant feedback to users about the presence of diseases or weeds. Furthermore, we focus on gathering and integrating diverse and extensive plant image datasets to enhance the performance of our models. Collaborating with experts and organizations to obtain high-quality labeled data is a critical aspect of this objective. Our ongoing commitment to continual model improvement, user education, and engagement ensures that PlantSnap remains an invaluable resource for plant health management.

System Overview


The methodology for developing the PlantSnap web application involves collecting a diverse dataset of plant images, creating a user-friendly interface with Next.js, and ensuring scalability and real-time analysis using cloud-based infrastructure. Emphasis is placed on user engagement and data quality for long-term success. Collaboration with agricultural experts ensures the integration of results with best practices, and thorough testing and continuous improvement ensure reliability and performance. Deployment on a reliable hosting service enables user accessibility, while staying updated with advancements in technology enhances the application's capabilities

Technologies

Flask

Flask is a lightweight and versatile Python web framework known for its simplicity and ease of use. It is widely used for building web applications, APIs, and microservices. Flask's minimalist design and extensive ecosystem of extensions make it a popular choice for web developers seeking flexibility and rapid development.

Tensorflow

TensorFlow is a free and open-source software library for dataflow and differentiable programming across a range of tasks. It is a symbolic math library, and is also used for machine learning applications such as neural networks.

Hugging Face

Hugging Face is a leading platform for natural language processing (NLP) and AI model development. It provides a wide range of pre-trained models and tools for NLP tasks, making advanced AI accessible to developers. Hugging Face has become a prominent hub for the NLP community, fostering innovation and collaboration in the field.

Keras

Keras is an open-source library that provides a Python interface for artificial neural networks. Keras acts as an interface for the TensorFlow library. Up until version 2.3 Keras supported multiple backends, including TensorFlow, Microsoft Cognitive Toolkit, R, Theano, and PlaidML.
